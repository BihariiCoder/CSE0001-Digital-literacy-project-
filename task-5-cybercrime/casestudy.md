# 💰 Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## 📌 Overview
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a rapidly growing cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into authorizing fraudulent transactions. These scams often exploit a lack of awareness and urgency.

## ⚙️ How It Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. The fraudster contacts the victim via call, SMS, or WhatsApp, posing as a bank official, employer, or buyer.  
2. They create urgency (e.g., “Your account will be blocked” or “You’ve received money”).  
3. The victim is asked to scan a QR code or approve a “collect request.”  
4. The victim unknowingly enters their UPI PIN, believing they are receiving money.  
5. Instead, the amount is debited from their account instantly.  

## 🎯 Target Audience
- College students and young users new to digital payments  
- Online sellers/buyers on platforms like OLX  
- Individuals unfamiliar with UPI security practices  

## ⚠️ Consequences
Victims may lose money instantly with limited chances of recovery if reporting is delayed. It can also lead to stress, loss of trust in digital platforms, and misuse of personal data for further scams.

## 🧾 Scenario (Fictional)
Ravi, a college student, tried selling his old laptop online. A “buyer” sent him a QR code claiming it was for receiving payment. After scanning and entering his UPI PIN, ₹8,000 was deducted instead of credited.

## 🚨 Key Lesson
Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Always verify requests and report fraud immediately via https://www.cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930.
